# Billing & Invoices FAQ

> Sample knowledge-base article — replace with your real billing policies.

## Billing cycle

Invoices are generated on the same day each month, based on the customer's
original activation date. Payment is due within 15 days of the invoice date.

## Accepted payment methods

- WE mobile wallet
- Credit/debit card via the WE app or website
- Bank transfer
- Cash at any WE store or authorized payment point (Fawry, Aman, etc.)

## Late payment

- **1–15 days late**: a reminder SMS/notification is sent; service continues
  normally.
- **16–30 days late**: a small late fee (5% of the invoice) may apply, and
  the customer receives a suspension warning.
- **30+ days late**: service may be suspended until the balance is settled.
  Reconnection after suspension is usually immediate once payment clears.

## Disputing a charge

If a customer believes a charge is incorrect (e.g., wrong plan price, a fee
they don't recognize, or a payment that isn't reflected):
1. Ask for the invoice date and the specific line item in question.
2. Do not promise a refund or credit — log a support ticket with issue type
   "Billing - Dispute" including the details, so the billing team can
   investigate and respond.

## Understanding common invoice line items

- **Plan subscription**: the recurring monthly fee for the customer's
  internet plan.
- **Add-ons**: static IP, extra mesh router, priority support, etc.
- **Prorated charges**: appear when a customer upgrades mid-cycle — this is
  the extra amount for the higher-tier plan for the remaining days for that
  cycle.
- **Late fee**: only appears if a previous invoice was paid after its due
  date.
- **VAT**: value-added tax applied per Egyptian tax regulations, shown as a
  separate line.

## Canceling a subscription

Cancellation requests must be logged as a support ticket with issue type
"Billing - Cancellation Request" so the retention team can follow up; agents
should not confirm a cancellation is finalized in the chat itself.
