# Board of Directors - Telecom Egypt

## Lobna Helal - Chairman of the Board of Directors
Ms. Lobna Helal assumed the position of Chairman of the Board of Directors of Telecom Egypt in March 2025, having previously served as an Independent Board Member in 2019.
Ms. Lobna Helal's experience in the banking sector spans over 30 years, where she most recently served as Deputy Governor of the Central Bank of Egypt for Monetary Stability Policies from 2011 to 2013 and 2015 to 2019, as the first woman to hold this position in the history of the Central Bank of Egypt. She was responsible for several key sectors of the Central Bank, including monetary policy, reserve management, markets, external relations, research, banking operations, information technology, payment systems, and banking sector reform. Ms. Lobna was responsible for developing, implementing, and enhancing a comprehensive monetary and economic reform program that spanned three years and successfully concluded in June 2019.
Since joining the Central Bank in 2004, Ms. Lobna prepared and implemented the banking sector development and restructuring program from 2004 to 2011 in two phases, which had a profound impact on developing and restructuring the banking sector. In addition to representing the Central Bank of Egypt in various local and international institutions as Deputy Governor, she was selected as an independent expert and Non-Executive Chairman of the Credit Guarantee Company, as well as an expert and Non-Executive Board Member in Nasser Social Bank, the National Investment Bank, the Mortgage Finance Fund, and the Social Housing Fund, as well as the Non-Executive Chairman of the Egyptian Mortgage Refinance Company.
Ms. Helal currently serves as the Non-Executive Chairman of City Edge Developments, Chairman of the Board of Trustees of the Foundation for Innovation for Sustainable Development, Board Member of National Bank of Egypt (London), Board Member of the Sovereign Fund of Egypt, experienced Board Member of the Social Housing Fund, Independent Board Member of Bank of Alexandria, and Board Member of the Al-Nidaa Foundation.
Ms. Helal represented the Central Bank of Egypt as Vice-Chair of the Board of Directors of the Alliance for Financial Inclusion (AFI) and also served as Vice-Chair of the Women's Financial Inclusion Support Committee within the same alliance. Prior to joining the Central Bank of Egypt, Ms. Lobna held the position of Executive Director at EFG Hermes, responsible for mergers and acquisitions and fixed-income debt instruments.
Ms. Lobna was ranked as the second most powerful woman in the Middle East and North Africa region according to Forbes magazine's assessment for the years 2016 and 2017 respectively, and the magazine also selected her as the third most influential woman in the government sector in the region for the year 2018.
Ms. Lobna holds a Master's degree in Economics from the American University in Cairo.

## Tamer El-Mahdi - Managing Director and CEO
Eng. Tamer El-Mahdi serves as the Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer of Telecom Egypt, boasting a professional experience spanning over three decades in leading strategic transformation, and developing, building, and operating telecommunications networks and digital infrastructure on regional and international levels.
El-Mahdi's expertise was built through a progressive and integrated career path that combined deep technical specialization with high executive and managerial capabilities. He began his career as a telecommunications engineer specializing in network engineering and communication systems, working at AT&T and Bell Labs, acquiring advanced technical experience in designing and operating strategic, highly reliable, large-scale, multi-technology, and multi-layered telecommunications networks. He then moved on to managing and executing major telecommunications projects before taking on leadership roles in designing telecommunications networks for major operators in the Middle East and North Africa through Lucent Technologies, where he led multidisciplinary teams and implemented wired and wireless telecommunications projects in a number of regional and international markets.
Transitioning to the operator side, he joined Orascom Telecom Holding, where he served as the Group Chief Technology and Information Officer (Group CTIO), leading multi-billion-dollar investments in mobile and fixed networks, and overseeing the launch and operation of telecommunications networks in multiple markets including Italy, Canada, Greece, Tunisia, Algeria, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Iraq, and North Korea, cementing his expertise in managing and operating multinational telecommunications groups.
He continued his career by taking on the role of CEO of Orascom Telecom Algeria (Djezzy), one of the largest mobile operators in North Africa and the Middle East, leading the company through a critical phase characterized by complex operational and regulatory challenges, successfully enhancing operational performance and institutional sustainability. This path culminated in his appointment as CEO of Orascom Telecom Media and Technology (OTMT), where he led a comprehensive strategic transformation and business diversification across multiple sectors and geographical regions in Europe, South Asia, and the Middle East, focusing on value maximization, governance enhancement, and building long-term growth platforms.
Alongside his executive career in major corporations, Eng. Tamer El-Mahdi plays a prominent entrepreneurial role, having founded and launched companies specializing in management and technical consulting and software development, providing advanced consulting services in the fields of operational and technical transformation, network design, and building institutional capabilities for telecommunications operators.
Eng. Tamer El-Mahdi holds advanced academic qualifications including a Master's degree in Telecommunications Engineering from California State University, a Master's in Project Management from George Washington University, a Master's in International Business Administration from Maastricht School of Management, in addition to a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Cairo University.

## Ahmed Abdel Rahman - Non-Executive Board Member
Major General Ahmed Saeed Youssef Abdel Rahman is one of the distinguished leaders in the field of signal communications in the Armed Forces, having served as Chief of Staff of the Signal Department and possessing extensive experience spanning over many years of professional military work. He holds a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Military Sciences, in addition to completing a number of advanced courses inside and outside Egypt, including the Staff Course at the Command and Staff College in Tunisia, the Fellowship of the Nasser Higher Military Academy, and the Senior Leaders Course. He is distinguished by a strong professional record reflecting his commitment, competence, and leadership ability in the various positions he has held.

## Tarek Abou Alam - Non-Executive Board Member
Tarek Abou Alam was appointed as a Board Member of Telecom Egypt, representing the government, in March 2021.
Abou Alam has over 25 years of experience in managing digital technology companies, achieving a distinguished set of accomplishments in the telecommunications and IT sectors in the Middle East and European markets. He is currently the Founder and Managing Partner of Glint Consulting, a consulting, investment management services, and startup development company.
Abou Alam has held numerous leadership positions in several companies throughout his career. He founded and managed Soficom Internet Services, which was later acquired by Bahrain Telecommunications Company (Batelco). He also served as Director of Planning and Business Intelligence at Wind Italy, an integrated telecommunications service provider, overseeing market analysis and product development during his tenure. He later joined Orascom Telecom Holding as Director of Broadband and Fixed Services Development.
Abou Alam subsequently took on the role of Senior Vice President for International and Operators at Telecom Egypt in 2009 before serving as the CEO and Managing Director of the company in 2012, playing a prominent role in leading the company during that period.
Abou Alam has also held the positions of Chairman and Board Member for several companies including Italia Online and IT Net in Italy, Inty in the UK, Telecom Egypt, Vodafone Egypt, TE Data, Xceed, Iqraaly, Wasla, and Soficom in Egypt, Tellas in Greece, Terrocon in the UAE, and MobiServe operating in Egypt, UAE, and Pakistan. He is a member of the Board of Trustees of the British University in Egypt.
Abou Alam holds a Bachelor's degree in Telecommunications and Electronics Engineering from Alexandria University.

## Tarek Tantawy - Non-Executive Board Member
Tarek Tantawy was appointed as a Board Member of Telecom Egypt, representing the government, in March 2021.
Tantawy has over 24 years of experience in the investment and management field and currently serves as the Co-Managing Director and Co-CEO of Palm Hills Developments.
Tantawy previously served as the Co-Managing Director and Co-CEO of CI Capital Group, where he contributed to developing the group's institutional work and transforming it into a leading company in financial services from his joining in 2015 until 2021.
Tantawy also joined Telecom Egypt from 2002 to 2011, holding several leadership positions including Managing Director and CEO between 2009 and 2011, after serving as Vice President for Financial and Administrative Affairs in 2007. He played a prominent role in offering Telecom Egypt shares in an international public offering, as well as executing the acquisition of a 45% stake in Vodafone Egypt.
Tantawy has also held several positions in other companies including Chief Financial Officer of the International Company for Agricultural Processing Projects "Beyti", Vice Chairman of the Investment Banking Board at Sigma Capital, and Senior Advisor in the Corporate Finance Department at FinRate Consulting, where he executed numerous distinct stock and bond offerings in local and global markets.
Tantawy currently holds board memberships in several companies listed on the Egyptian Stock Exchange and closed companies, including Palm Hills Developments, Taaleem Management Services, and Incolease for Financial Leasing. He previously held board memberships in several companies including CI Capital Holding for Financial Investments, Corplease for Financial Leasing, Vodafone Egypt Telecommunications, and TE Data.
Tantawy holds a Master's degree in Business Administration from Heriot-Watt University in the UK and a Bachelor's degree in Construction Engineering from the American University in Cairo, in addition to being a Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA).

## Hany El Samra - Non-Executive Board Member
Hany El Samra was appointed as a Board Member of Telecom Egypt in October 2022, representing the government.
El Samra possesses over 26 years of experience in the field of Human Resources in the banking sector in Egypt and the Middle East. He currently serves as the Chief Human Resources Officer at Banque Misr since 2018, effectively leading the bank's HR strategy.
Prior to joining Banque Misr, El Samra held leadership positions in three of the most prominent banks at the regional and international levels. He began his career at "Citibank - Egypt" in 1999, progressing through leadership roles to become Vice President of Human Resources for the bank's branch in Kuwait in 2007. He then assumed the role of Vice President of Human Resources at "Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank - Egypt" in 2009, later moving to become Head of Human Resources at "Citibank - Kuwait" in 2011. El Samra also served as General Manager of Human Resources and Development at "Burgan Bank - Kuwait" in 2016.
He also serves as a Board Member at Misr Abu Dhabi Real Estate Investments and Chairman of the Human Resources Committee at the Federation of Egyptian Banks.
El Samra has obtained several prestigious professional certifications, including the Certified Director of Corporate Practice (CDCP) certificate in cooperation with the International Finance Corporation in August 2022, the Executive Leadership Program from Harvard University in June 2022, and a Certified Assessor certificate from SHL UK. He has also completed advanced programs in job analysis and evaluation from the HAY Group.

## Islam Zekry - Non-Executive Board Member
Mr. Islam Zekry was appointed to the Board of Directors representing the Egyptian government in March 2025.
With over 20 years of experience in the banking sector, Islam Zekry serves as the Group Chief Financial Officer at Commercial International Bank (CIB), where he leads the financial and strategic operations of the bank. Zekry has a proven track record of enhancing and improving financial performance, driving growth, and fostering innovation, backed by his extensive experience in global markets, regulatory environments, banking sector developments, and mergers and acquisitions. Through his leadership, he continues to promote CIB's long-term strategic goals and deliver value to stakeholders.
Previously, Zekry achieved a historic milestone as the first Chief Data Officer for CIB in Egypt, spearheading the bank's data transformation journey. Under his leadership, CIB established advanced capabilities in data warehousing, analytics, and AI-powered customer insights. This pioneering achievement garnered global recognition, with both London Business School and Harvard Business School featuring CIB as the first Middle Eastern case study in their curricula.
Zekry is known as a thought leader in the field of digital finance and the modern economy. With his innovative approach, he contributes to the global digital transformation through his role as a member of the Smart Africa Steering Committee. He is an active member of the EU-AU Digital Economy Task Force and a member of the Digital Transformation Committee of the World Economic Forum. He was also recently appointed to Egypt's first Artificial Intelligence Council, which shapes the national vision and executive strategy for AI.
Thanks to his extensive international presence and experience across Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, Zekry serves as a non-executive board member in several financial and technological companies in the region. He is a member of the Chartered Institute of Management Accountants in the UK and a member of the Fitch Institute of Quantitative Finance in London.
Zekry holds a Ph.D. in Banking Performance Analytics from Westbrook University, Minnesota, an MBA from the University of Chicago, and a Bachelor of Commerce (Accounting major) from Zagazig University. In addition to his academic degrees, he has obtained numerous professional certifications in finance, data management, and information systems from leading global institutions.

## Mohamed Barakat - Independent Board Member
Mr. Mohamed Barakat assumed a position on the Board of Directors of Telecom Egypt in March 2017 as a Non-Executive Board Member, and became an Independent Board Member in March 2025.
With extensive banking experience spanning over 40 years, Barakat currently serves as the Chairman of the Board of Directors of the National Bank of Egypt (Non-Executive) and a Board Member of the Financial and Technological Services Subfund of the Sovereign Fund of Egypt since April 2021.
Mr. Barakat has delivered a strong track record of achievements in senior management positions. He served as Vice Chairman and Managing Director of the Arab International Bank from 2014 to 2022. He was the Chairman of the Board of Directors - Banque Misr from 2002 to 2014, one of the largest Egyptian banks.
Barakat has held numerous leadership positions including Chairman of the Board of Directors - Banque Misr Liban, Banque Misr Europe - Frankfurt, Banque du Caire, and was appointed Chairman of the Federation of Egyptian Banks, and Chairman of the Union of Arab Banks, after serving as Vice Chairman of the Union of Arab Banks.
He served as a member of the Board of Directors of the Central Bank of Egypt from 2003 to 2011 as a member of the Banking Reform Committee and the Audit Committee, a Board Member of the Egyptian Banking Institute, a Board Member in the Anti-Money Laundering Unit, and a Board Member and elected Vice Chairman of Cairo Amman Bank in Jordan.
Globally, he was appointed as a Regional Board Member of Visa International (Central Europe, Middle East and Africa region) from 2003 to 2008, and a member of the Advisory Board of Visa International from 2008 to 2010. He was a member of the Advisory Board - MasterCard International (Africa and the Middle East) from March 2011 to 2020.
On the non-banking level, he served as Chairman of the Board of Directors - the International Company for Touristic Investments (the owner of the Conrad Hotel - Cairo), and the Suez Canal Company for Technology Settling (the owner of October 6 University), and a Board Member there from 2015 to 2022. He also served as a member of the Board of Trustees there, in addition to being a Board Member of the Holding Company for EgyptAir.
Mr. Mohamed Barakat holds a Master's degree in Business Administration from the University of San Francisco, USA, and a Bachelor's degree in Accounting from Alexandria University.

## Girgis Abd El-Shahid - Independent Board Member
Mr. Girgis Abd El-Shahid assumed the position of Board Member of Telecom Egypt in March 2025.
Mr. Girgis Abd El-Shahid possesses extensive experience in providing corporate legal consultancy and dispute resolution, particularly commercial arbitration, along with a strong background working with numerous sectors including logistics, aviation, pharmaceuticals, food, construction, energy, finance, securities offering, telecommunications, intellectual property, and banking affairs. Mr. Girgis Abd El-Shahid serves as the Managing Partner at Shahid Law Firm in Cairo.
Mr. Abd El-Shahid is considered one of the youngest Egyptian lawyers to handle high-profile arbitration cases in Egypt and the Middle East, having gained a prestigious reputation due to his expertise in complex multi-party arbitration cases and international law. Alongside his legal practice, he served as a member of the International Court of Arbitration for two consecutive terms ending in June 2024, and was also a member of the Advisory Committee of the Cairo Regional Centre for International Commercial Arbitration (CRCICA). He began his professional career working as a public prosecutor.
Mr. Abd El-Shahid holds a Master's degree in International Legal Studies from Georgetown University Law Center in the USA, in addition to a Bachelor of Laws (LLB) from Cairo University, and a Bachelor's degree in Economics and Business Administration from the American University in Cairo.

## Akef El Maghraby - Independent Board Member
Mr. Akef El Maghraby assumed the position of Board Member of Telecom Egypt in March 2025.
Mr. Akef El Maghraby possesses over 25 years of experience in the fields of banking and finance, with a proven track record in all banking activities, financial strategies, and economic development. El Maghraby currently serves as the CEO and Managing Director of Suez Canal Bank.
Prior to assuming this position, El Maghraby served as Vice Chairman of Banque Misr, where he played a pivotal role in managing several key sectors, including corporate banking, treasury, international business, retail and SME banking, investment, Islamic finance, global transaction services, regional operations, branches, and financial inclusion. His leadership and strategic vision contributed significantly to the bank's expansion and modernization, particularly in the area of digital transformation.
Before joining Banque Misr, El Maghraby served as Head of Corporate and Investment Banking at Citibank Egypt, where he successfully doubled the asset base and increased revenues by 50% in 2015. During his career spanning more than a decade at Citibank, he led numerous major financial transactions and provided financing and advisory solutions to several leading corporations and government entities across various sectors, including iron and steel, chemical industries, energy, and others.
In addition to his executive roles, El Maghraby currently serves as Chairman of Misr Digital Innovation and Misr Capital, and is a Board Member of Banque Misr Liban, Misr Insurance Company, the Egyptian Banking Institute, EgyptAir Holding Company, the Information Technology Industry Development Agency (ITIDA), Africa 50, CI Capital, and the Export Development Bank of Egypt. He is also a member of the American Chamber of Commerce (AmCham).
Mr. Akef El Maghraby holds a Master's in Business Administration (MBA) and a Bachelor's degree in Accounting and Finance from the American University in Cairo.

## Hossam Seifeldin - Independent Board Member
Mr. Hossam Seifeldin assumed the position of Board Member of Telecom Egypt in March 2025.
Hossam Seifeldin possesses nearly 30 years of experience in the technology sector and global business strategies.
